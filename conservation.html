1. Drought Intensity and Frequency

A. Drought Analysis :- Trends Over Time: The analysis of meteorological data from 1990 to 2020 revealed a significant increase in the frequency and intensity of droughts across the selected states. For example, Rajasthan experienced an average of 2.5 severe drought years per decade, compared to only 1 in the previous decades. Similarly, Gujarat showed a notable increase in the duration of drought conditions, with some regions experiencing droughts lasting up to 18 months.



2. Socio-Economic Impact : Agricultural Productivity

Interviews with local farmers revealed a direct correlation between drought events and reduced crop yields. For instance, in Maharashtra, many farmers reported yield losses of 30-50% for staple crops like wheat and pulses during drought years. This decline has exacerbated food insecurity and increased the reliance on government aid.

3. Status and Extent of Desertification: Geospatial Analysis

Soil Quality Assessment: Field surveys indicated significant soil degradation, characterized by reduced fertility and increased salinity levels. Soil samples from Rajasthan showed a 15% decrease in organic matter content, which directly impacts agricultural productivity and ecosystem health.

4. Analysis of Remediation Methods

A. Traditional Remediation Practices
(i) Rainwater Harvesting: The study found that traditional practices such as rainwater harvesting have been successfully implemented in various communities. In Rajasthan, johads (traditional earthen check dams) have been effective in recharging groundwater and improving water availability during dry spells. Communities reported a 25-30% increase in water availability for agriculture following the construction of johads.
(ii) Agroforestry: Integration of trees with agricultural crops was another successful practice observed. Farmers practicing agroforestry noted improved soil health and increased biodiversity, which contributed to enhanced resilience against drought.
B. Non-Traditional Remediation Methods
(i) Modern Irrigation Techniques: The adoption of drip irrigation systems in Gujarat has shown promising results, leading to water savings of up to 50%. Farmers who implemented these systems reported better crop yields and reduced reliance on groundwater. However, the high initial costs of installation remain a barrier for widespread adoption.
(ii) Community-Based Approaches: The involvement of local communities in designing and implementing remediation strategies was critical to their success. Case studies highlighted the effectiveness of community-led initiatives in restoring degraded lands, as these efforts leveraged local knowledge and fostered a sense of ownership.
